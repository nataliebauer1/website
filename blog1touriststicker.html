A Fieldguide to Sticker Genres

Introduction

When talking to people about stickers, they always have something in mind - they might have recently gotten a cute one from a friend, picked one up at an animal rights booth, or saw an offensive one in the streets. However, while normally around the size of the palm of the hand, sticker is not just sticker. Most of them cater to a collective identity. This means that they try to communicate something to an audience that is only able to decipher its claims if it is familiar with the visual and linguistic symbolism used. If successfull, the audience has some sort of emotional reaction to the sticker, e.g., in political dis-/agreement or wanting to purchase an item. Additionally, just like any other form of art and communication, stickers cannot be placed strictly into one category; instead certain genres can be observed. To start things off, you will get an introduction to the different types of stickers out there before we move on to topics from related fields, such as street art. The Tourist 

24 March 2026

Like a guest who has made themselves at home and now refuses to leave, it clings to some lamppost in some city, brought by someone who has left again already. A stylised instagram handle at a viewpoint somewhere in Queensland, Australia, a fading “Nett hier, aber waren Sie schon mal in Baden-Württemberg?”1-sticker on a lamp pole bin in Singapore. The tourist sticker leaves a mark, most of the time showing a symbol typical for a traveller's hometown, like a coat of arms, or plainly spelling its name out. While hardly ever displaying authorship, it whispers, “I was here”, even when the person who placed it is long gone. However, thanks to travel influencers and other guides, tourists tend to end up in similar places, leaving many “I was here”s for the next visitor to line up. From experience (and maybe placing a lot of good will in people), they hardly ever get overstickered. Instead, a mural by visitors, who, if not anything else, claimed a tiny part of the place they came to, emerges.

1

"Nice here, but have you ever been in Baden-Württemberg?" is a tourism campaign by the German state of Baden-Württemberg that has enjoyed unbroken popularity among German holidaymakers for years.
