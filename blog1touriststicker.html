An Atlas of Stickers

To start things off, you will get an introduction to the different types of stickers out there before we move on to topics from related fields, such as street art. To that end, one type of stickers will be featured each week.
When talking to people about stickers, they always have something in mind - they might have recently gotten a cute one from a friend, picked one up at an animal rights booth, or saw an offensive one in the streets. However, while normally around the size of the palm of the hand (Awcock, 2021), sticker is not just sticker. They are identity-building, meaning that they try to communicate something to an audience that is familiar with the visual and linguistic symbolism used on the sticker, as well as the language, in order to decipher its claims. Additionally, just like any other form of art and communication, they cannot be placed strictly into one category, but certain genres can be observed:

The Tourist 

Like a guest who has made themselves at home and now refuses to leave, it clings to some lamppost in some city, brought by someone who has left again already. A “Nett hier, aber waren Sie schon mal in Baden-Württemberg?” at a viewpoint somewhere in Queensland, Australia, a fading Real Madrid sticker on a rubbish bin in Singapore. The tourist sticker leaves a mark, most of the time showing a symbol typical for a traveller's hometown or plainly spelling it out. While hardly ever displaying authorship, it whispers, “I was here”, even when the person who placed it is long gone. Thanks to travel influencers and other guides, tourists tend to end up in similar places, leaving many “I was here”s for the next visitor to line up.
